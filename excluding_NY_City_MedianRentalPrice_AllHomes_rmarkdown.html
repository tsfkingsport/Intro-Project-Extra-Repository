City Median Rental Prices (Excluding New York City)

This is going to explain what I am doing to examine the median rental prices across every city in the Springboard city list except for New York due to the amound of smaller cities that are listed as being in the New York City metropolitan area.

After I feel confident about what I have done with rent and house values outside of New York City then I will examine New York city and figure out how to deal with the data there.

After this I created a character vector containing the names of the cities I wanted to examine.

city_list <- c("San Francisco","Boston", "Chicago","Los Angeles", "Washington",
               "Seattle","Atlanta", "Austin", "Raleigh", "Durham", "Philadelphia",
               "Dallas", "Denver", "Detroit", "Minneapolis", "Saint Paul", "San Diego",
               "Houston")

Next was to pipe my dataset into a filter command.

rent_tibble <- full_rent_df %>% filter(RegionName  %in% city_list)

This filter command gives a table of 20 rows and 113 variables. Two of the rows at the end are cities named Washington that are not in the District of Columbia so those need to be removed.

rent_tibble <- rent_tibble[1:18, ]

Unfortunately all of the dates have a X at the start of the date for reasons that I cannot figure out. Next I removed these characters from the column names. This code does have the drawback of removing any X characters from other column names but my dataset does not have any other X characters in the column names. If for some reason it did I am not sure what my solution would end up being.

names(rent_tibble) <-gsub("X","", names(rent_tibble))

I wanted to change the columns into a datetime format with ymd(). I am still not entirely sure what the practical benefit of it is for my project but it seemed like a good idea at the time. To accomplish this I needed to replace the . character with - because - is more friendly to ymd()

names(rent_tibble) <-gsub("\\.","-", names(rent_tibble))

Next I removed 4 columns that did not have any direct application to what I wanted to do. I also wanted to change variables when moving onto the next part.

Z <- rent_tibble[,-c(2:5)]

After this I made the data more tidy by gathering the dates to make the data more narrow.

Z<- gather(Z, key = "date", value = "rent", - 1)

The data was tidier but I wanted the data ordered by city and change the variable name to something more intuitive.

rent_df<-Z[order(Z$RegionName),]

After this the data was ready for some basic visualization.

rent_df %>% ggplot(aes(x = date, y = rent, colour = RegionName)) +
